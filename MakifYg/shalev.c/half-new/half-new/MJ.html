Michael Jordan

Michael Jordan, also known as “His Airness,” is considered by many as the greatest basketball player of all time. He was born on February 17, 1963, in Brooklyn, New York. Jordan played 15 seasons in the National Basketball Association (NBA), first for the Chicago Bulls and later for the Washington Wizards. Throughout his career, he has achieved numerous accolades and has set numerous records that have yet to be broken. Jordan’s professional career began in 1984 when he was drafted third overall by the Chicago Bulls. In his rookie season, he was named Rookie of the Year, averaging 28.2 points per game. Throughout the 1980s and 1990s, Jordan dominated the league, winning six NBA championships, five Most Valuable Player (MVP) awards, ten scoring titles, and was named to the All-Star team 14 times. He is one of only four players to have won 10 or more scoring titles in their career. Jordan’s clutch performances and his ability to lead his team to victory in critical moments have cemented his legacy as one of the greatest basketball players in history. He was known for his signature moves, such as the dunk from the free-throw line and his fadeaway jump shot. His scoring ability and leadership skills made him a fan favorite and a player that was feared by opponents. In 1991 and 1992, Jordan led the Bulls to the first of two three-peats, where they won three consecutive NBA championships. He was named the Finals MVP for all six of his championship runs.
