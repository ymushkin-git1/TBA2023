Spaghetti Bolognese 1 lb. spaghetti, 1 lb. ground beef, 1 onion, 2 cloves of garlic, 1 can of diced tomatoes, 1 tsp. salt, 1 tsp. pepper 1. Cook spaghetti according to package instructions.
2. In a separate pan, brown the ground beef.
3. Add diced onion and minced garlic to the beef.
4. Once the onion is translucent, add the canned tomatoes, salt, and pepper.
5. Let the sauce simmer for 15 minutes.
6. Serve the sauce over the cooked spaghetti. 		Calories: 500, Fat: 15g, Protein: 30g, Carbohydrates: 60g
Grilled Chicken Salad 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, 1/4 cup olive oil, 2 cloves of garlic, 1 tsp. salt, 1 tsp. pepper, 4 cups mixed greens, 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese, 1/4 cup chopped walnuts, 1/4 cup balsamic vinaigrette 1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
2. In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
3. Brush the chicken with the marinade and grill for 6-8 minutes per side, or until fully cooked.
4. Allow the chicken to rest for 5 minutes before slicing.
5. In a large bowl, toss together the mixed greens, feta cheese, chopped walnuts, and balsamic vinaigrette.
6. Add the sliced chicken to the salad and toss gently to combine. 		Calories: 350, Fat: 20g, Protein: 30g, Carbohydrates: 10g
Veggie Stir Fry 1 lb. mixed vegetables, 1 tbsp. vegetable oil, 2 cloves of garlic, 1 tbsp. soy sauce, 1 tsp. sugar, 1 tsp. corn starch 1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over high heat.
2. Add the mixed vegetables and stir fry for 3-4 minutes, or until tender.
3. In a small bowl, mix together the minced garlic, soy sauce, sugar, and corn starch.
4. Pour the sauce over the vegetables and stir to coat.
5. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened. 		Calories: 200, Fat: 10g, Protein: 4g
Chicken stroganoff 2 tbsp olive oil, 4 skinless chicken breasts, cut into chunks, 2 onions, finely chopped, 4 garlic cloves, crushed, 1 tbsp sweet paprika, 400ml chicken stock, 4 tsp Dijon mustard, 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 200g soured cream or crème fraiche, a large handful of parsley, chopped, rice mash or pasta, to serve 1.Heat half the oil in a frying pan and fry the chicken pieces over a medium high heat, stirring frequently. until golden brown. Season and transfer to a plate.
2.Heat the remaining oil and lower the heat to medium. Fry the onion for 6-8 mins until softened but not golden, then stir in the garlic and paprika. Fry for a minute until fragrant, then pour in the stock, and add the Dijon and Worcestershire sauce. Tip in the seared chicken with any resting juices, and simmer for 5-6 mins until reduced slightly and the chicken is cooked through.
3.Over a low heat, stir in the soured cream until just combined to create a creamy sauce, and it’s just started to simmer. Scatter over the parsley and serve with rice, mash or pasta, if you like. 		Calories: 425, Fat: 23g, Protein: 43g, Carbohydrates: 11g
Steak burrito bowl 1 fillet steak, approx 250g, 1 sachet fajita seasoning, 2 tbsp olive oil, 250g Mexican-style rice pouch, 2 roasted red peppers, sliced, 1 lemon, ½ juiced, ½ cut into wedges, ½ small pack parsley, roughly chopped, to serve, yogurt, to serve 1. Coat the steak with half of the fajita seasoning and 1 tbsp of the olive oil. Heat a frying pan over a high heat and add the steak, cook for 2 mins on each side for medium rare, remove from the pan and set aside.
2.Turn down the heat to medium and add the remaining oil to the pan. Tip in the rest of the fajita seasoning, the rice and red peppers. Stir well so everything is coated and cook for 2 mins, until hot through. Add a splash of water if it starts to stick. Add the lemon juice, stir through half the parsley and season.
3.Cut the steak into strips. Tip the rice into bowls and top each with the steak and a dollop of yogurt. Scatter with parsley and serve with a lemon wedge. 		Calories: 549, Fat: 24g, Protein: 32g, Carbohydrates: 49g
Oven-baked pork chops 1kg new potatoes, halved, 4 tbsp olive oil, 6 garlic cloves, bashed, small handful of thyme, 6 tbsp honey, 6 tbsp wholegrain mustard, 4 thick-cut bone-in pork chops (about 250g each) 1.Heat the oven to 200C/fan 180C/gas 6. Toss the potatoes, oil, garlic and thyme on a shallow roasting tray and mix will with your hands to coat in the oil. Season well. Roast for 25 mins until the potatoes are just turning tender when pierced with a knife.
2.Whisk the honey and mustard together in a small bowl. Season the pork chops with salt and pepper, then brush with half the marinade. Nestle into the potatoes on the tray, and bake for another 10 mins before turning the chops and spooning over the remaining glaze. Continue to bake for 10 more mins until cooked through. 		Calories: 805, Fat: 38g, Protein: 46g, Carbohydrates: 67g
Creamy salmon, leek & potato traybake 250g baby potatoes, thickly sliced, 2 tbsp olive oil, 1 leek, halved, washed and sliced, 1 garlic clove, crushed, 70ml double cream, 1 tbsp capers, plus extra to serve, 1 tbsp chives, plus extra to serve, 2 skinless salmon fillets, mixed rocket salad, to serve (optional) 1. Heat the oven to 200C/180C fan/gas 6. Bring a medium pan of water to the boil. Add the potatoes and cook for 8 mins. Drain and leave to steam-dry in a colander for a few minutes. Toss the potatoes with ½ of the oil and plenty of seasoning in a baking tray. Put in the oven for 20 mins, tossing halfway through the cooking time.
2.Meanwhile, heat the remaining oil in a frying pan over a medium heat. Add the leek and fry for 5 mins, or until beginning to soften. Stir through the garlic for 1 min, then add the cream, capers and 75ml hot water, then bring to the boil. Stir through the chives.
3.Heat the grill to high. Pour the creamy leek mixture over the potatoes, then sit the salmon fillets on top. Grill for 7-8 mins, or until just cooked through. Serve topped with extra chives and capers and a salad on the side, if you like. 		Calories: 714, Fat: 52g, Protein: 39g, Carbohydrates: 20g
